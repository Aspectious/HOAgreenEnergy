Geothermal Heat Pump System

Overview Benefits Planning Installation Maintenance

Overview

Geothermal heat pumps use the heat of the earth instead of the outside air temperature to heat, cool, and supply the house with hot water. They are superior to air-source heat pumps because they don't depend on the outside air temperature. In addition, they are very efficient and produce renewable energy.

Benefits

Geothermal heat pump systems have several benefits, including that they:

  1. Are more efficient and quieter than air-source heat pumps
  2. Don't depend on the temperature of the outside air
  3. Reduce energy consumption
  4. Need little maintenance
  5. Last longer than air-source heat pumps
  6. Allow for design flexibility
  7. Usually provide break even on the installation cost during their lifetime

Planning

To plan your geothermal heat pump system, you should define the heating and cooling efficiency, the system's economics, and your site's attributes. As the US has constant shallow ground temperatures, geothermal heat pump systems can virtually be installed and used anywhere in the country. However, you should work with your installer to determine the exact specifications of your system based on your site.

Installation

A qualified installer should install a geothermal heat pump system. To find one near you, reach out to your local utility company, the International Ground Source Heat Pump Association, or the Geothermal Exchange Organization. Often, there are incentives for installing a renewable energy source in your area that can decrease the upfront installation cost.

Maintenance

Properly maintaining your geothermal heat pump system ensures maximum energy efficiency. You should follow the manufacturer's instructions for the system's maintenance. Annually, you should have your system serviced by a professional technician.
