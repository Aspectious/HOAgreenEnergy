Wind Energy

Overview Benefits Planning Installation Maintenance

Overview

Wind energy produces electricity by using wind turbines to turn the kinetic energy of moving air into electricity. This is called rotational energy, which is then converted to electricity in the generator. It is one of the largest renewable energy sources in the United States.

Benefits

  • Wind energy is particularly advantageous in remote areas, as it can be utilized in places that aren't connected to the electricity grid.
  • Wind energy is mostly consistent. In relatively windy places, wind is guaranteed to be regular. Although during some seasons there might be less wind, it doesn't depend on the time of day, unlike solar energy. Therefore it is consistent in the medium to long term.
  • Wind energy is very efficient. The efficiency of wind energy, which is between 40 to 50%, is very near to the theoretical maximum, 59%.
  • Wind energy is not very expensive to install or maintain. It takes quite less time to install (between 2-24 months depending on the size) and many government incentives offered for wind energy have caused the expense to decrease dramatically.
  • Wind energy requires very little land as most of the structure extends vertically. Barely any land is used for the base when compared to solar energy plants that cover a lot of ground.
  • The environmental impact is very low. Wind energy solely use resources for the creation and installation of wind turbines, which makes it the most environmentally friendly option compared to other renewable energy sources. Also, certifications are required in some places to install wind turbines to ensure there is no negative impact on the animals and plants of that location.

Planning

Before installing a wind energy system, you need to determine if the system would be suitable for your location and your energy consumption.

First, you need to estimate your location's annual wind speed or wind resource. Before determining a specific estimate, you can look at this map to determine if your area is in a suitable wind resource region. If you are in an appropriate wind resource region, the next step is to work with a licensed wind turbine system installer. Your installer will use multiple ways to determine a more specific estimate of your site's wind resource, including: Airport wind speed data, vegetation flagging, measurement system, and local small wind system data.

Next, you should research local zoning ordinances and restrictions from neighborhood covenants. Then, you should work with your installer to examine the economics of a small wind turbine system to determine whether wind energy is a good option for your site. This website can help you get started on some of these estimates.

Installation

You should have a licensed installer install your small wind energy system to ensure the maximum life of the system. The system can last up to 20 years if installed correctly and maintained. The installation process includes:

  1. Finding the best location for the system.
  2. Choosing the correct size turbine and tower.
  3. Deciding if you want to connect the system to the electric grid.

Maintenance

Annual maintenance is recommended to maximize the wind energy system's life. Your installer may offer a service plan or recommend someone else.
