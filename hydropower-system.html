Microhydropower System

Overview Benefits Planning Installation Maintenance

Overview

Microhydropower systems are a great renewable energy source for homes with running water on the property. Microhydropower systems typically generate a maximum of 100 kilowatts, but a system that produces 10 kilowatts should be enough for a large home.

Benefits

Microhydropower systems have many benefits. They are reliable as the power generation is constant. Also, they can produce enough energy to power a relatively large home. Finally, the systems generate renewable energy and, therefore, are helping the environment.

Planning

A reliable flowing water source on your property is necessary to install a microhydropower system. In addition to assessing water flow and vertical drop, a thorough evaluation of a should go over power output, economics, permits, and water rights. To approximately estimate the power output of a typical micro-hydro system (50-70% efficient), consider the available "net head" (vertical drop minus piping losses) and water flow rate. Multiply net head (in feet) by flow (in gallons per minute) and divide by 10 to get wattage. This simple calculation is a starting point for assessing your site's potential. Installation

Once you have determined that a microhydropwer system works for you, you can start the installation process. It would be best to work with a licensed installer to set up the system properly. Micro hydro power systems cost between $1,500 and $4,000 per kilowatt installed capacity, although the cost depends also on your site. Although this might be expensive, it will be worth it in the long run. Plus, there may be financial incentives for using a renewable energy system in your area, which can decrease the cost.

Maintenance

Hydroelectric systems don't require much maintenance as they last a very long time before needing any repairs. Contact a professional for any repairs or annual maintenance.
